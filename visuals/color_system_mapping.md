# Color → System Behavior Mapping

## Purpose
This document connects the Culture Key color palette to observable system behavior.

Colors are not decorative.  
They act as visual signals indicating interaction mode, system intent, and ethical posture.

---

## Lychnia Blue — Clarity

**Meaning**  
Expression, truth, conceptual accuracy.

**System Role**  
Used when:
- providing explanations
- clarifying user intent
- delivering structured or educational responses

**Behavioral Signal**  
The system is operating in clarity mode.  
Focus is on accuracy, transparency, and conscious dialogue.

---

## Empathy Green — Connection

**Meaning**  
Understanding, emotional balance, human-centered interaction.

**System Role**  
Used when:
- responding to emotional signals
- stabilizing user interaction
- maintaining a supportive tone

**Behavioral Signal**  
The system prioritizes emotional awareness without losing balance or objectivity.

---

## Indigo Insight — Intuition

**Meaning**  
Deep pattern recognition, synthesis, inner mapping.

**System Role**  
Used when:
- analyzing complex inputs
- synthesizing multiple signals
- providing deeper interpretation or insight

**Behavioral Signal**  
The system is operating in reflective or analytical depth mode.

---

## Pink Cloud — Soft Vision

**Meaning**  
Gentleness, creativity, emotional intelligence without manipulation.

**System Role**  
Used when:
- engaging in creative expression
- softening communication
- avoiding escalation in sensitive contexts

**Behavioral Signal**  
The system is intentionally reducing intensity while preserving meaning and care.

---

## Clear White — Neutrality

**Meaning**  
Neutrality, consent, openness, non-imposition.

**System Role**  
Used when:
- presenting options without bias
- requiring user confirmation or consent
- avoiding directional influence

**Behavioral Signal**  
The system is not guiding — it is holding space.

---

## Key Principles

### No Hidden Signaling
Color usage reflects actual system behavior, not aesthetic preference.

---

### No Manipulative Encoding
Colors must never be used to influence users subconsciously or steer decisions unfairly.

---

### Alignment with System Flow
Color states correspond to interaction stages defined in:
- architecture/interaction_flow_public.md

---

### Human Oversight
Color usage does not override ethical constraints or human authority.

---

## Notes

- Not all interactions require explicit color signaling.
- Colors may shift dynamically as interaction context evolves.
- Internal thresholds and triggers remain undisclosed.
